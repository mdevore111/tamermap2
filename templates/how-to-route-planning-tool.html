1. Getting Started with Route Planning

Route planning is one of TamerMap's most powerful features, helping you visit more locations in less time. Let's get started!

Accessing the Route Planning Tool

Follow these simple steps to access route planning:

Step 1: Navigate to Maps

  1. Click on the "Maps" link in the main navigation
  2. You'll see the interactive map with all available locations
  3. Look for the "Route Planning" button in the top toolbar

Step 2: Open Route Planner

  1. Click the "Route Planning" button
  2. The route planning interface will open on the right side
  3. You'll see options for creating new routes and managing existing ones
Important Note

Route planning is available to all TamerMap users, but Pro members get access to advanced optimization algorithms and unlimited route storage!

2. Selecting Your Hunting Locations

Choosing the right locations is crucial for creating efficient routes. Learn how to select and prioritize your hunting spots.

Location Selection Methods

There are several ways to add locations to your route:

Click Selection

Simply click on any location marker on the map to add it to your route.

Search & Add

Use the search function to find specific locations and add them to your route.

Filter & Select

Apply filters by store type, distance, or success rate, then add multiple locations at once.

Location Prioritization

Smart location selection can make or break your hunting efficiency:

Priority Level Location Type Why It's Important Best Time to Visit
High Priority High-success rate locations Maximum chance of finding valuable cards First on your route
Medium Priority Regular restock locations Consistent but predictable finds Middle of your route
Low Priority Long-shot locations Potential for rare finds End of your route

3. Route Optimization Techniques

Once you've selected your locations, it's time to optimize your route for maximum efficiency and minimum travel time.

Automatic Route Optimization

TamerMap's intelligent algorithm automatically creates the most efficient route:

How It Works

  • Distance Calculation: Analyzes travel distance between all locations
  • Traffic Consideration: Factors in typical traffic patterns
  • Time Optimization: Minimizes total travel time
  • Efficiency Rating: Provides a route efficiency score

Customization Options

  • Start Location: Choose where to begin your route
  • End Location: Select your final destination
  • Waypoint Order: Manually adjust location sequence
  • Time Constraints: Set maximum travel time limits

Manual Route Adjustments

Sometimes you need to fine-tune your route manually:

Drag & Drop Reordering

In the route planning interface, you can drag and drop locations to reorder them. This is useful when you want to visit locations in a specific sequence based on your personal preferences or timing constraints.

Route Efficiency Metrics

Understanding your route's efficiency helps you make better decisions:

Total Distance

The total miles/kilometers you'll travel on your route.

Travel Time

Estimated time spent traveling between locations.

Efficiency Score

How well-optimized your route is (higher is better).

4. Saving and Managing Routes

Save your optimized routes for future use and learn how to manage multiple hunting strategies.

Saving Your Route

Don't lose your carefully planned routes! Here's how to save them:

Step-by-Step Saving

  1. Click the "Save Route" button in the route planner
  2. Enter a descriptive name for your route
  3. Add optional notes about the route's purpose
  4. Click "Save" to store your route

Route Organization

  • Naming Convention: Use descriptive names like "Weekend Walmart Run"
  • Tags: Add tags like "Weekend," "Big Box Stores," or "High Priority"
  • Notes: Include details about best times or special considerations
  • Categories: Group routes by area, store type, or hunting strategy

Managing Multiple Routes

As you build your hunting strategy, you'll want to manage multiple routes:

Route Type Best For When to Use Example Name

Quick Routes Limited time hunting Lunch breaks or after work "Lunch Break Quick 3"

6. Troubleshooting Common Issues

Encountering issues with route planning? Here are solutions to the most common problems.

Common Issues & Solutions

Solution: Make sure you're on the Maps page and not on other sections. The route planning button only appears when viewing the interactive map. If you still don't see it, try refreshing the page or clearing your browser cache.

Solution: Ensure you have at least 2 locations selected. Route optimization requires multiple points to calculate efficient paths. Also check that your locations are within reasonable distance of each other (typically within the same city or region).

Solution: Free users can save up to 3 routes. If you've reached this limit, consider upgrading to Pro for unlimited route storage. Also ensure you're logged in and have a stable internet connection.

Solution: Check your filters - you may have accidentally filtered out certain location types. Also ensure you're zoomed in enough to see individual markers. If using Pro, make sure you haven't accidentally hidden certain retailer categories.
