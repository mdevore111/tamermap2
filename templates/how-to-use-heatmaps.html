1. What is the Heatmap Feature?

The heatmap feature is TamerMap's visual representation of hunting activity across different locations, helping you identify where other hunters are finding success.

Understanding Heatmap Basics

Think of the heatmap as a "success radar" for Pokemon card hunting:

What It Shows

  • Activity Levels: How frequently locations are visited
  • Success Patterns: Where hunters find valuable cards
  • Popular Areas: High-traffic hunting zones
  • Hidden Gems: Less popular but potentially profitable locations
  • Trending Locations: Recently discovered hot spots

Why It's Valuable

  • Data-Driven Decisions: Base hunting choices on real activity
  • Efficiency Boost: Focus on proven successful locations
  • Competitive Advantage: Discover less crowded spots for better finds
  • Time Savings: Avoid low-success areas
  • Success Rate Increase: Learn from community experience
Important Note

The heatmap shows activity patterns, not guaranteed finds. High activity usually indicates better inventory or success rates, but individual results may vary.

2. How to Access Heatmaps

Accessing the heatmap feature is simple and intuitive. Follow these steps to start using this powerful tool.

Step-by-Step Access Guide

Step 1: Navigate to Maps

  1. Click on the "Maps" link in the main navigation
  2. Wait for the interactive map to load completely
  3. Look for the heatmap toggle button in the toolbar

Step 2: Enable Heatmap

  1. Click the heatmap toggle button (usually looks like a flame icon)
  2. The map will overlay with colored activity data
  3. You can adjust the time range using the slider (1-60 days)

Heatmap Controls and Options

Once enabled, you'll have several options to customize your heatmap view:

Time Range Slider

Adjust the time range for heatmap data (1-60 days).

Time Range

Select activity data from different time periods (day, week, month).

Layer Toggle

Turn heatmap on/off without losing your current map view.

Pro Tip

Pro users can access advanced heatmap features and detailed activity breakdowns by location type!

3. Reading Heatmap Data

Understanding how to read heatmap data is crucial for making informed hunting decisions. Learn the color coding and what different patterns mean.

Color Coding System

The heatmap uses a color-coded system to represent activity levels:

Color Activity Level What It Means Hunting Strategy
Red Very High Extremely active location with frequent visits High priority - visit early and often
Orange High Active location with regular hunting activity Good priority - reliable hunting spot
Blue Medium Moderate activity with occasional visits Medium priority - worth checking periodically
Gray Low Minimal activity or newly added location Low priority - check when convenient

Understanding Activity Patterns

Beyond just colors, the heatmap reveals important patterns:

Concentration Patterns

  • Hot Spots: Small, intense red areas indicate very successful locations
  • Activity Zones: Larger colored areas show popular hunting regions
  • Activity Trails: Linear patterns suggest popular travel routes
  • Activity Clusters: Multiple nearby hot spots indicate rich hunting areas

Temporal Patterns

  • Peak Times: Activity levels vary throughout the day
  • Weekly Patterns: Some locations are busier on weekends
  • Seasonal Changes: Activity levels may vary by season
  • Trending Locations: New hot spots that are gaining popularity
Reading Strategy

Start by identifying the reddest (hottest) areas on your map. These represent the most active hunting locations and typically offer the best chances for finding valuable cards. Then look for patterns - are there clusters of hot spots? Activity trails? This information helps you plan efficient hunting routes.

4. Finding Hot Spots

Hot spots are the gold mines of Pokemon card hunting. Learn how to identify, analyze, and capitalize on these high-activity locations.

Identifying Prime Hot Spots

Not all red areas are created equal. Here's how to identify the best hot spots:

Characteristics of Great Hot Spots

  • Consistent Activity: Red color that doesn't fade over time
  • Focused Intensity: Small, concentrated red areas (not spread out)
  • Strategic Location: Easy access, good parking, convenient hours
  • Store Type Match: Aligns with your hunting preferences

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Fading Activity: Red areas that are becoming orange/blue
  • Scattered Patterns: Large, diffuse red areas (may indicate past success)
  • Inconsistent Timing: Activity that varies wildly day-to-day
  • Poor Accessibility: Difficult to reach or inconvenient hours

Analyzing Hot Spot Quality

Use these criteria to evaluate potential hot spots:

Quality Factor High Quality Low Quality Why It Matters
Activity Consistency Steady red over time Fading or fluctuating Indicates reliable success
Location Concentration Focused, intense red Spread out, diffuse Shows specific success spots
Accessibility Easy access, good hours Difficult to reach Affects hunting frequency
Store Type Matches your preferences Not your preferred type Aligns with hunting strategy

Hot Spot Discovery Strategies

Use these techniques to find the best hunting locations:

Discovery Techniques

Zoom In: Get close to see individual location markers within hot spots. Check Time Ranges: Compare daily, weekly, and monthly views to see which locations maintain consistent activity. Cross-Reference: Use the heatmap with other TamerMap features like success rate data and user reviews to validate hot spots.

5. Strategic Planning with Heatmaps

Transform heatmap data into actionable hunting strategies. Learn how to plan efficient routes and maximize your success rate.

Route Planning with Heatmap Data

Use heatmap information to create optimal hunting routes:

Route Optimization Strategy

  • Start with Hot Spots: Begin your route at the reddest locations
  • Cluster by Color: Group nearby locations of similar activity levels
  • Time Your Visits: Visit high-activity spots during peak times
  • Include Variety: Mix hot spots with medium-activity locations

Timing Optimization

  • Peak Activity Times: Visit hot spots when activity is highest
  • Competition Avoidance: Time visits to minimize competition
  • Restock Alignment: Coordinate with known restock schedules
  • Weather Considerations: Plan indoor routes for bad weather

Building Your Hunting Strategy

Create a comprehensive strategy based on heatmap analysis:

Primary Targets

Focus on consistent red hot spots for maximum success probability.

Secondary Locations

Include orange and blue areas to maximize route efficiency.

Exploration Spots

Check gray areas occasionally for hidden gems and new discoveries.

Adapting to Heatmap Changes

Heatmaps evolve over time. Stay flexible and adapt your strategy:

Adaptation Strategies

Monitor Changes: Check heatmaps regularly to spot new hot spots or fading locations. Adjust Routes: Modify your hunting routes based on changing activity patterns. Test New Areas: Don't get stuck in old patterns - explore emerging hot spots. Seasonal Adjustments: Be aware that activity patterns may change with seasons or events.
