Route Planning Fundamentals

Effective route planning is the foundation of successful Pokemon card hunting. A well-planned route can double or triple your success rate.

The 5-Step Route Planning Process

  1. Define Your Hunting Area - Choose a geographic region based on your available time and travel preferences
  2. Research All Locations - Use TamerMap to identify every potential card location in your area
  3. Prioritize by Value - Rank locations by their potential for valuable finds
  4. Optimize Travel Path - Plan the most efficient route between locations
  5. Build in Flexibility - Allow time for unexpected discoveries and delays
Pro Tip: Start with smaller routes (5-8 locations) and gradually expand as you gain experience and confidence.

Key Route Planning Principles

Do This
  • Plan routes in loops or circuits
  • Start early in the morning
  • Group locations by proximity
  • Account for traffic patterns
  • Have backup locations ready
Avoid This
  • Backtracking unnecessarily
  • Rush hour travel
  • Overpacking your schedule
  • Ignoring restock schedules
  • Forgetting to eat/rest

Optimization Techniques

Advanced optimization techniques can significantly improve your route efficiency and success rate.

Geographic Clustering

Group locations by geographic proximity to minimize travel time:

  • Neighborhood Clusters - Hit all locations in one area before moving to the next
  • Highway Corridors - Plan routes along major roads and highways
  • Mall Complexes - Target multiple stores in shopping centers
  • Strip Malls - Visit adjacent stores in the same complex

Time-Based Optimization

Schedule your route based on optimal timing for each location:

Morning Route (8-11 AM)
  • Fresh inventory locations
  • Low competition stores
  • Early-opening retailers
  • Garage sales and flea markets
Afternoon Route (1-4 PM)
  • Mid-day restock locations
  • Lunch break opportunities
  • Less crowded stores
  • Estate sales and auctions

Dynamic Route Adjustment

Stay flexible and adapt your route based on real-time conditions:

  • Inventory Updates - Adjust route if a location gets fresh stock
  • Traffic Conditions - Reroute to avoid delays
  • Unexpected Opportunities - Add locations discovered during your hunt
  • Time Constraints - Drop low-priority locations if running behind

Advanced Strategies

Master these advanced techniques to become a route planning expert:

Multi-Day Route Planning

For serious hunters, plan routes across multiple days:

  • Day 1 - High-priority locations and fresh inventory spots
  • Day 2 - Secondary locations and backup spots
  • Day 3 - Exploration of new areas and long-shot locations

Seasonal Route Adaptation

Adjust your routes based on seasonal patterns:

Spring Routes
  • Focus on garage sales
  • Estate sale locations
  • Tax season sales
  • Spring cleaning finds
Holiday Routes
  • Gift return locations
  • Post-holiday sales
  • Holiday-themed stores
  • Special event venues

Competition Analysis

Stay ahead of other hunters by analyzing competition patterns:

  • Peak Hours - Avoid times when other hunters are most active
  • Popular Locations - Hit high-competition spots early or late
  • Alternative Routes - Find less obvious paths to the same locations
  • Timing Advantage - Use restock schedules to arrive first

Success Tips

Expert tips to maximize your route planning success:

Start Small and Scale Up

Begin with 3-5 locations and gradually expand your routes as you gain experience and confidence.

Keep Detailed Records

Track which locations produce results, when they restock, and what you find. This data improves future route planning.

Build Relationships

Get to know store employees and regular customers. They often have valuable insider information about restocks and special events.

Stay Flexible

The best route is one that adapts to changing conditions. Always have backup plans and be ready to pivot when opportunities arise.
